Newcomer Workforce Integration Platform
Lead Product Designer (UX)

Overview
Designed a 0→1 digital platform to help newcomers to Canada navigate complex employment systems and enter the workforce faster. Led end-to-end product design across research, strategy, and delivery for a national initiative.

Problem
Newcomers struggled with fragmented and confusing employment systems:
    • Information spread across multiple disconnected platforms
    • Unclear next steps led to high drop-off rates
    • Mismatch between user expectations and real hiring processes
Impact: Low program completion and delayed workforce integration.

Role
Lead Product Designer (UX)
    • Owned end-to-end design (discovery → delivery)
    • Led research, synthesis, IA, and prototyping
    • Collaborated with policy stakeholders, employment experts, and engineers

Users
    • Primary: New immigrants navigating employment systems
    • Secondary: Employment counselors supporting job placement

Approach
Research
    • Conducted 25+ user interviews with newcomers and advisors
    • Identified core pain points: confusion, overwhelm, lack of guidance
Key Insight:
Users didn’t need more information—they needed clear, guided pathways

Problem Framing
Reframed from:
“Provide employment resources”
To:
“Guide users through a structured journey from arrival → job readiness → employment”

Information Architecture
    • Consolidated fragmented systems into a step-based journey model
    • Introduced progressive disclosure to reduce cognitive load
    • Mapped key decision points across the user lifecycle

Design & Prototyping
    • Created wireframes → high-fidelity prototypes in Figma
    • Designed guided workflows (onboarding, job pathways, document prep)
    • Simplified complex processes into clear, actionable steps

Testing & Iteration
    • Conducted usability testing with target users
    • Iterated on navigation clarity and task completion

Solution
A guided digital platform that:
    • Personalizes user journeys based on goals and background
    • Breaks complex systems into clear, sequential steps
    • Integrates fragmented resources into a single experience

Impact
    • ↑ Program completion rate by ~30%
    • ↓ User drop-off during onboarding
    • Improved job-readiness outcomes for 500+ users
    • Scaled across multiple regions in Canada

Key Decisions
    • Prioritized clarity over completeness
    • Replaced static content with interactive journeys
    • Designed for users with low system familiarity

Challenges & Tradeoffs
    • Balancing simplicity with completeness of government requirements
    • Navigating ambiguity across policy stakeholders
    • Designing for diverse language and cultural contexts

What This Demonstrates
    • 0→1 product thinking
    • Systems design for complex ecosystems
    • Research-driven decision making
    • Measurable user impact
    • Cross-functional collaboration in ambiguous environments

Next Steps / Improvements
    • Expand personalization using behavioral data
    • Introduce multilingual support at scale
    • Optimize flows based on long-term retention metrics

    • Key product screens (onboarding, pathway flow)

Trauma-Informed Clinical Examination System
Lead Product Designer (UX)
Dalhousie University, Dept. of Medicine | 2018–2024

Overview
Redesigned how medical professionals conduct high-risk, trauma-sensitive physical examinations by developing a scalable, evidence-based training system used across clinical environments.
This work transformed fragmented, inconsistent practices into a standardized, user-centered protocol that improves both patient outcomes and clinical efficiency.

Problem
Survivors of assault frequently experience secondary trauma during medical examinations due to:
    • Inconsistent clinician approaches
    • Poor communication and lack of transparency
    • Examination methods that prioritize procedure over patient experience
System impact:
    • Increased patient distress and non-compliance
    • Inefficient examinations and higher error risk
    • Lack of standardized, research-backed protocols

Role
Lead Product Designer (UX)
    • Led end-to-end system design (research → framework → delivery)
    • Conducted qualitative research across patients and clinicians
    • Translated behavioral insights into scalable clinical protocols
    • Partnered with medical faculty and frontline practitioners

Users
    • Primary: Trauma survivors undergoing clinical examination
    • Secondary: Physicians, nurses, first responders
    • Tertiary: Medical educators and institutions

Approach
Research & Insight Generation
    • Synthesized patient feedback via confidential channels and community outreach
    • Conducted deep literature review across trauma-informed care and clinical practice
Key Insight:
Clinical effectiveness was not limited by technical skill—but by interaction design at the human level

Behavioral Design Framework
Identified micro-interactions that significantly impact patient perception and outcomes:
    • Touch mechanics: Back-of-hand vs palm contact materially affected perceived safety
    • Language systems: Terminology, tone, and naming conventions influenced trust and compliance
    • Agency design: Introducing patient participation reduced anxiety and improved exam accuracy

System Design
    • Translated insights into repeatable clinical protocols
    • Designed structured examination flows integrating communication, consent, and participation
    • Developed modular training programs adaptable across multiple medical domains

Iteration & Optimization
    • Continuously evaluated and refined programs on a 6-month cycle
    • Expanded system across specialties (cardiovascular, urological, emergency care, pharmacy simulations)

Solution
A scalable trauma-informed examination system that:
    • Standardizes clinician behavior through evidence-based interaction models
    • Embeds patient agency directly into clinical workflows
    • Aligns medical efficiency with human-centered care

Impact
Clinical Performance
    • ↓ Examination time from ~6 minutes → ~2 minutes (~65% reduction)
    • ↓ Training time from 90 minutes → 60 minutes (~33% improvement)
    • ↑ Consistency and accuracy of clinical execution
Patient Outcomes
    • Increased patient comfort, trust, and compliance
    • Reduced physical and psychological stress during examinations
Adoption
    • Adopted by hundreds of medical professionals across disciplines
    • Established as a repeatable model for trauma-informed care delivery

Key Decisions
    • Prioritized behavioral precision over procedural complexity
    • Designed for emotional safety as a core system requirement
    • Balanced clinical rigor with human-centered interaction design

Challenges & Tradeoffs
    • Standardizing human behavior across diverse practitioners
    • Balancing efficiency with sensitivity in time-critical environments
    • Navigating ambiguity in subjective patient experience metrics

What This Demonstrates
    • Systems thinking applied to high-stakes, real-world environments
    • Ability to translate qualitative human insight into scalable frameworks
    • Designing for edge cases and vulnerable populations
    • Measurable impact on both efficiency and human outcomes

Next Steps
    • Digitize protocol delivery into interactive clinical tools
    • Integrate real-time feedback systems for continuous improvement
    • Expand into broader healthcare systems and policy-level adoption

Mobile Trading Card Game (TCG) Platform
Lead Product Designer (UX)
Tobenstein Technologies | 2023

Overview
Designed a mobile-first digital trading card game platform focused on acquisition, engagement, and long-term retention. Led end-to-end product design, from core gameplay systems to monetization and release strategy.

Problem
The digital TCG market is highly competitive and saturated, with user expectations shaped by established ecosystems.
Key challenges:
    • High barrier to entry for new users
    • Low retention due to complexity or lack of novelty
    • Difficulty balancing innovation with familiar gameplay patterns
Business risk: Failure to achieve early retention and repeat engagement leads to rapid product churn.

Role
Lead Product Designer (UX / Product)
    • Owned 0→1 product design (game systems, UX, engagement loops)
    • Conducted competitive analysis across leading TCG platforms
    • Designed gameplay mechanics, progression systems, and user flows
    • Defined product strategy, release cadence, and engagement model

Users
    • Primary: Mobile gamers (casual → mid-core)
    • Secondary: Franchise fans (high emotional investment)

Approach
Competitive & Behavioral Analysis
Analyzed top-performing TCGs to identify:
    • Push factors (acquisition): familiarity, IP recognition, accessibility
    • Pull factors (retention): progression, collection systems, social play
Key Insight:
Users expect predictable systems with controlled novelty—innovation succeeds only when layered on top of familiar mechanics

System Design
Core Gameplay Loop
    • Collect → Customize → Battle → Reward → Progress
Designed to:
    • Minimize onboarding friction
    • Maximize short-session engagement (<5 minutes per match)
    • Encourage repeat play through reward systems

Engagement & Retention Systems
    • Structured release cadence (monthly / seasonal content drops)
    • Card rarity and progression systems to drive long-term investment
    • Social and competitive mechanics to reinforce retention

UX & Interaction Design
    • Simplified complex gameplay into intuitive mobile-first interactions
    • Designed for fast comprehension and low cognitive load
    • Balanced depth (strategy) with accessibility (casual entry)

Solution
A scalable TCG platform that:
    • Reduces onboarding friction while preserving strategic depth
    • Drives engagement through short, repeatable gameplay loops
    • Balances innovation with established player expectations

Impact
    • Delivered full product design system, gameplay framework, and go-to-market strategy
    • Defined retention model based on proven behavioral patterns
    • Created a product designed for high replayability and long-term engagement

Key Decisions
    • Prioritized engagement loops over feature complexity
    • Anchored innovation within familiar interaction patterns
    • Designed for session flexibility (short bursts vs deep play)

Challenges & Tradeoffs
    • Balancing originality with market expectations
    • Designing for both casual and invested users
    • Aligning product depth with mobile usability constraints

Outcome & Reflection
The product was not launched due to funding constraints.
However, the project demonstrated:
    • Strong product intuition around engagement and retention systems
    • Ability to design within competitive, expectation-heavy markets
    • End-to-end ownership from concept to strategy

What This Demonstrates
    • Product thinking (0→1 + growth systems)
    • Behavioral design for engagement and retention
    • Ability to operate in competitive consumer markets
    • Translating domain expertise into scalable product design

Next Steps
    • Prototype and validate engagement loops through live testing
    • Introduce analytics to measure retention and monetization
    • Expand into cross-platform ecosystem (mobile + web)

Global eLearning Platform (TalkNow)
Lead UX Researcher & Product Designer
Tobenstein Technologies | 2020

Overview
Led UX research and product design for a large-scale eLearning platform targeting a multi-country user base across East and Southeast Asia. Focused on building a scalable system that supports diverse user needs across age groups, roles, and cultural contexts.

Problem
Designing a unified platform for a highly diverse user base introduced major challenges:
    • Wide variation in age, goals, and learning styles
    • Conflicting needs across instructors, learners, and subscribers
    • High expectations for usability in an already mature product category
System risk:
Failure to balance user needs would result in low adoption and poor engagement at scale

Role
Lead UX Researcher & Product Designer
    • Led a team of 12 researchers across multiple regions
    • Defined user personas, behavioral models, and product requirements
    • Translated research into product design strategy and UX systems

Users
    • Learners: Engagement, usability, satisfaction
    • Instructors: Interactivity, teaching effectiveness
    • Subscribers (buyers): Security, ROI, platform reliability

Approach
Multi-Market Research
    • Developed personas across Japan, Thailand, China, and South Korea
    • Identified key behavioral differences across regions and demographics

Systems Thinking
Mapped overlapping and conflicting priorities across user groups:
    • Security vs accessibility
    • Engagement vs simplicity
    • Standardization vs localization
Key Insight:
Success depended on designing a system that balances competing needs without over-optimizing for any single group

Product Strategy
    • Adopted established UX patterns to reduce learning curve
    • Focused innovation on interaction quality and engagement, not novelty
    • Designed for scalability across markets and user types

Solution
A multi-role eLearning platform that:
    • Supports diverse user needs through role-based experiences
    • Maintains usability through familiar interaction patterns
    • Scales across regions with minimal friction

Impact
    • Contributed to launch in a highly competitive market (~2.2B potential users across 17 countries)
    • Established foundational UX systems and personas for product development
    • Delivered scalable research framework for future iterations

Challenges & Tradeoffs
Ethical Conflict (Critical Learning)
    • Encountered significant concerns around user privacy and data protection
    • Advocated for stronger privacy standards in a low-regulation environment
    • Navigated misalignment between business priorities and user protection

Outcome & Reflection
The product launched but the company did not sustain long-term success.
Key takeaway:
Strong UX systems must be supported by ethical product decisions and sustainable business practices

What This Demonstrates
    • Designing at global scale across diverse markets
    • Managing multi-stakeholder complexity
    • Balancing competing user needs within a single system
    • Strong ethical judgment in product design

Next Steps
    • Strengthen privacy-first design frameworks
    • Introduce governance models for ethical product decisions
    • Validate systems through longitudinal user data
